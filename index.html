Bio

I have been working as a Machine Learning Engineer at the Innovation Center of Yinhua Fund Management Co., Ltd, a leading public fund in mainland China since the summer of 2020.

I completed my Master's degree in Applied Statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor in 2020, supervised by Kerby Shedden. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from the School of Mathematics and Statistics at Wuhan University.

My current interests focus on (1) general theories of Machine Learning and Deep Learning methods, including generative models and reinforcement learning; (2) analysis of large language models, such as effective fine-tuning models for specific domains, distillation of LLMs and evaluating and assessing the performance of LLMs; (3) computational finance, specifically the analysis of financial time series data.

Not only working in the industry has fostered resilience within me, enabling me to maintain a steadfast approach even in the face of obstacles, it also has heightened my understanding of the importance of delving deep into technology. Simultaneously, the continuous advancements and innovations in the field of technology have ignited a passion within me, compelling me to become a part of the community of explorers.

Currently, I am actively seeking opportunities to pursue a Ph.D.

Courses

Featured Courses include:

EECS 595/LING 541 Natural Language Processsing
Presented by Prof. Joyce Y. Chai
This course introduces modern Natural Language Processing (NLP) technologies, which involve computational models and algorithms for processing human languages. It covers topics such as syntax, semantics, discourse, deep learning for NLP, and their applications in information extraction, machine translation, and dialogue systems.
STATS 503 Statistical Learning
Presented by Prof. Ji Zhu
This course uses R as the programming language and provides a comprehensive introduction to, as well as practical implementation of, classic statistical learning algorithms. It covers regression methods and classification methods in supervised learning, as well as clustering algorithms in unsupervised learning.
EECS 598 Special Topic: Computational Data Science
Presented by Prof. Raj Rao Nadakuditi
This course utilizes Julia as the programming language and extensively covers the principles and broad applications of Singular Value Decomposition (SVD). Topics include classification, principal component analysis, image denoising, image alignment, video processing, and more.
