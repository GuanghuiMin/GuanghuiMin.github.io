Bio

I have been working as a Machine Learning Engineer at the Innovation Center of Yinhua Fund Management Co., Ltd, a leading public fund in mainland China since the summer of 2020.

I completed my Master's degree in Applied Statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor in 2020, supervised by Kerby Shedden. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from the School of Mathematics and Statistics at Wuhan University.

My current interests forcus on (1) analysis of large language models, such as effective fine-tuning models for specific domains, distillation of LLMs and evaluating and assessing the performance of LLMs; (2) applications of generative models in practical scenarios; (3) computational finance, specifically the analysis of financial time series data.

Though working in the industry has fostered resilience within me, enabling me to maintain a steadfast approach even in the face of obstacles, it also has heightened my understanding of the importance of delving deep into technology. Simultaneously, the continuous advancements and innovations in the field of technology have ignited a passion within me, compelling me to become a part of the community of explorers.

Currently, I am actively pursuing opportunities to pursue a Ph.D., and I have prepared my CV and SOP accordingly.

Prospective Students

I'm recruiting PhD students to start Fall 2023 at the University of Washington! I'm especially looking for students with a background and/or interest in social network analysis, machine learning, causal inference, or natural language processing, and are passionate about using computational methods to study how online social platforms can be reimagined to enable better conversations, bridge political divides, and reduce the spread of misinformation. If you are interested, please apply to the UW iSchool PhD program and mention me in your application. If you have any questions, feel free to reach out by email!
